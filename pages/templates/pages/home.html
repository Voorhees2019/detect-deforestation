The protection of the natural environment from criminal encroachments and the prevention of environmental crimes is one of the most pressing problems of our time. Scientific and technological progress and increased anthropogenic pressure on the natural environment inevitably lead to an exacerbation of the ecological situation: the reserves of natural resources are depleted, the environment is polluted, the natural connection between man and nature is lost, aesthetic values are lost, the physical and moral health of people deteriorates, the economic and political struggle for commodity markets and living space.

One of the most important objects of nature is the forest. This component of the environment contains a huge resource potential and performs various ecological, economic, cultural and recreational functions.

Illegal deforestation is one of the main threats to forests around the world. Considering that illegal deforestation is concentrated in forests, which are characterized by a rich species diversity of plants and animals, their ecological consequences are catastrophic. Illegal logging has become a serious problem for forests in recent decades. The volume of illegally cut timber has been growing recently due to the lack of proper control by the state.

Illegal deforestation also has social consequences: forest taxes are not paid from illegally cut timber, which leads to a decrease in tax collections received from the use of forest resources.
